PLAY AND REFLECT

INTRO













“Play and reflect” is the project I am working on during my first year (officially Premaster) at Sint
Lucas Antwerpen School. My course in Visual Arts in Socio Political Context helped us to open our minds
to the different practices of involving people, making us reflect on the different forms that a Design
project can take, not only as a physical or digital product, but also through activities and workshops.
The project is mainly born thanks to the conversations with the people I had the opportunity to meet
in this new beginning in Antwerp and who in most cases are, like me, “new” in the city. Talking to
people from different parts of the world made me reflect on how different the “obstacles” to arriving
in a city can be, often simply due to different nationalities, or how much in a world where everything
seems “within reach” we are still so tied to certain stereotypes.

In this digital age in which facial recognition claims to define human beings by collecting data,
we realize that in reality we still know little about the people we share this planet with.

Collecting, defining, classifying: these are the main phases that facial recognition systems use to describe
the inhabitants of the world, to associate them, to try to understand who to pay attention to or not.
If the intent is to make the world safer, in reality these means only strengthen our bond to certain prejudices.













Speaking with the people I interviewed during the research phase of my project,
some of them pointed out how often they have been judged or victims of disrespectful
behavior simply for their appearance, or at other times later
have made it known what their nationality was.

"But you do have wi-fi in Ecuador?" "Do you bring with you weapons when you are in Colombia?"
"Italians are addicted to mom" "You don't look like a Cuban" "Spanish people are always lazy"
are just some of the phrases that the people I spoke with heard themselves say.

How can we try to get away from these preconceptions?
And how do we make appearance something small and irrelevant?














"It's natural to have prejudices, the real problem is
that people often don't break free from them, and when you try
to explain things as they are, they don't pay attention."

KEYWORDS

My project therefore focuses on creating greater mutual awareness, showing how people who do not know each other are able
to discover a lot about each other's background and the origins of others starting from topics related to
their daily life or their memories. Given the intentions, I wanted to make sure that the project had certain key points:
collaboration, interaction, inclusivity, co-creation, reaction, comparison.













The project therefore had a crucial phase: a workshop during which, in random pairs,
the participants had to carry out 10 different activities to get to know each other
in alternative and engaging ways. The activities were the pretext to stimulate broader
conversations, some more curious, others more personal,others more profound.


These are the titles of the activities:

1- Bring me to your place of the heart

2 - Your search produced your personal results

3 - Life in a pocket

4 - Sounds like home

5 - Ceci n’est pas un object

6 - What’s for dinner?

7 - I'm not a robot

8 - Words to be destroyed

9 - Bookmark

10 - Polaroid from XXXX

BRING ME TO YOUR PLACE OF THE HEART

In the first activity "Bring me to your place of the heart", each member of the couple shared with
each other the google maps link of a place that is important to him in the world. With the street
view function, he then guided him through the streets to find out, letting the other person
move with the mouse, but directing him to specific points and telling him anecdotes about it or
simply why that place is so important to him.

YOUR SEARCH PRODUCED YOUR PERSONAL RESULTS

In “Your search produced your personal results”, the participants have defeated the typical disappointment
we have when we want to show from the internet where we come from and the google results
show images that do not do it justice. With a little html trick, the participants changed
the images by inserting instead of the ones they didn't like some photos that best describe
their place for them: not only its most beautiful landscapes, but also the places they are most fond of.
The result is a very personal kind of virtual collage.

LIFE IN A POCKET

“Life in a pocket” led participants to share the contents of their wallets (excluding money, which
was not important). We all have something that we collect in our wallet, that we want to keep as a souvenir,
or that we have simply forgotten or "lost" inside.
People explained the stories of these little souvenirs.

SOUNDS LIKE HOME

"Sounds like home" was an activity that obviously had a sound aspect.
Participants have shared recordings from their phone, links to important songs
or searched YouTube or the various online sound archives for the sounds they are related to.

CECI N'EST PAS UN OBJET

"Ceci n’est pas un objet" asked the participants to show an object that is important to them
(either physically or through a photo). People have chosen a word that for them that object
represents and recreated it using the object itself and explaining why.

WHAT'S FOR DINNER?

"What's for dinner" was a moment of sharing one's favorite recipe (whether it was typical of one's country
or not). The participants shared the story, the ingredients, explained if they are now all easy to find
in the new place they live and shared tips…
in most cases they ended up organizing a dinner!

I'M NOT A ROBOT

"I'm not a robot". Captcha are asking us to prove that we are not robots by selecting photos of
buses, cars, boats. Is being able to do so what is enough to consider oneself human beings?
What are the main qualities that a person needs, from your point of view, to be considered “human”?
The participants share their opinions with each other and wrote them down.

WORDS TO BE DESTROYED

"Words to be destroyed". Inspired by Man Ray's medium of art "Objet to be destroyed", the participants
wrote on a piece of paper the judgments that have particularly offended them in the course
of their lives. Participants were free to share these words or not. Once written,
they simply had to destroy the paper as they pleased.

BOOKMARK

“Bookmark” Does a bookmark serve to remind you where you have arrived, or does it serve to remind
you of what you still want to achieve? The participants created a bookmark for each other, writing
on the one hand the things that the partner says he was satisfied with having achieved, on the other
hand the goals he would like to achieve.

POLAROID FROM XXXX

“Polaroid” Participants describe one of their best childhood memories as their partner tries to reproduce
them via a sketch on a fake Polaroid photo.

OUTPUT













All the activities helped the participants to get to know each other better,
each activity was in fact the beginning of more in-depth discussions that brought out
a lot of content about people. The "collected" material has nothing to do
with their appearance as is the case with Facial Recognition.
The material obtained from the workshop also gave rise to a sort of collection,
but a collection of"real human beings", physically represented by vinyls.

OUTPUT

Each of the vinyls I created summarizes a small story of the participants who took part in my workshop,
the 10 different activities gave the name to the 10 vinyl tracks. The activities were the pretext
to stimulate broader conversations, some more curious, others more personal, others more profound.
The content of the vinyls tells a lot about these people without the need to show their faces properly.
Reflected images do not reveal to us a copy of reality, but they help us to observe it
in a different way and to open ourselves to change perspective.
